## Classic Problems

### Easy
1. **Two Egg Problem**: Two eggs, 100 floors. Find minimum number of drops to find breaking floor.
2. **Bridge Crossing**: 4 people, 1 bridge, 1 flashlight. Cross in minimum time.
3. **Water Jugs**: 3L and 5L jugs. Measure exactly 4L.

### Medium
1. **Pirates and Gold**: 5 pirates, 100 gold coins. Democratic distribution, maximize your share.
2. **Monty Hall**: Three doors, one prize. Switch or stay?
3. **100 Lockers**: 100 students, 100 lockers. Which lockers are open?
4. **Burning Ropes**: Two ropes, each burns in 60 minutes (non-uniform). Measure 45 minutes.

### Hard
1. **25 Horses**: Find top 3 fastest horses with minimum races (5 horses per race, no timer).
2. **Light Bulb**: You're downstairs, 3 switches, 1 bulb upstairs. One trip to identify which switch.
3. **Russian Roulette**: Gun with 2 bullets in consecutive chambers. Spin or shoot? Why?
4. **Ants on Triangle**: 3 ants on triangle vertices, random direction. Probability of collision?

## Approach Strategy
1. **Clarify**: Ask questions, state assumptions
2. **Simple Cases**: Start with smaller numbers
3. **Patterns**: Look for patterns or symmetries
4. **Work Backwards**: Sometimes easier from the end
5. **Eliminate**: Rule out impossible scenarios
6. **Check**: Verify your answer makes sense

## Tips for Interviews
- Think out loud
- Draw diagrams
- Consider edge cases
- Don't give up too quickly
- Ask clarifying questions
- Be creative and flexible in thinking
